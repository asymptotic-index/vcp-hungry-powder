# Failure Modes
Hungry Powder — Venus Cooling Project

---

## Purpose of This Document

This document enumerates the **primary failure modes** of the Hungry Powder
concept. Each failure mode represents a distinct way in which the idea fails,
even when granted perfect fictional materials and ideal deployment.

---

## Failure Mode 1 — Planetary-Scale Mass Handling

**Description:**  
The amount of material that must be processed and stored is planetary in scale.

**Cause:**  
- Total atmospheric mass of ~4.8 × 10²⁰ kg
- Near-total removal required for pressure reduction

**Effect:**  
- Solid residue comparable to planetary geological layers
- Global burial depths exceeding 100 meters

**Classification:**  
Mass dominance failure

---

## Failure Mode 2 — Pressure–Temperature Decoupling

**Description:**  
Lowering pressure does not significantly reduce surface temperature.

**Cause:**  
- Persistent solar input
- Stored atmospheric and surface heat
- Greenhouse-independent heating

**Effect:**  
- Venus remains lethally hot after atmospheric removal

**Classification:**  
Energy dominance failure

---

## Failure Mode 3 — Chemical Heat Release

**Description:**  
Atmospheric binding reactions release additional heat.

**Cause:**  
- Exothermic sequestration chemistry
- No heat export mechanism

**Effect:**  
- Net thermal load increases
- Cooling progress is reversed

**Classification:**  
Thermodynamic backfire

---

## Failure Mode 4 — Nitrogen Residual Barrier

**Description:**  
Removing CO₂ alone leaves unsafe pressure levels.

**Cause:**  
- ~3.3 bar nitrogen residual
- Need for additional fictional material (NHP)

**Effect:**  
- Escalating material requirements
- Diminishing returns

**Classification:**  
Partial-solution failure

---

## Failure Mode 5 — Energy Balance Persistence

**Description:**  
Venus continues absorbing solar energy regardless of atmospheric composition.

**Cause:**  
- Proximity to the Sun
- Lack of solar control mechanisms

**Effect:**  
- Temperatures rebound or stabilize at high levels

**Classification:**  
System boundary failure

---

## Failure Mode 6 — Long-Term Irreversibility

**Description:**  
Once material accumulates, reversal is impossible without releasing stored energy.

**Cause:**  
- Permanent chemical binding
- No safe disposal path

**Effect:**  
- Planetary state locked into a new, unstable configuration

**Classification:**  
Irreversibility failure

---

## Summary

> Hungry Powder fails because it solves the *visible* problem,  
> not the *governing* one.

Venus is controlled by energy, not chemistry.
